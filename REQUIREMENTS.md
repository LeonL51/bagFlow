The Problem

Key Features 

1. Smart Spending Analysis
 The app organizes expenses into clear categories and identifies spending patterns over time. Students can easily see where their money is going each month and how their abits compare across weeks or semesters. This feature helps users recognize areas of overspending that may otherwise go unnoticed.
    a. Expense Categorization
    The app sorts student expenses into clear categories such as food, transport, rent, entertainment, and school supplies.

    b. Pattern Tracking
    It monitors spending habits over time, allowing students to compare weekly, monthly, or semester-based expenses.

    c. Overspending Insights
    The app identifies areas where users may be spending too much, helping them notice financial habits they might otherwise miss.

2. Predictive Budget Forecasting
 Based on recorded income and past spending behavior, the app estimates how much money a student is likely to have remaining by the end of the month. If spending trends indicate a potential shortfall, the app provides early warnings so users can adjust before financial stress occurs.
    a. Income and Spending Review
    The app uses recorded income and past expense data to understand the student’s financial situation.

    b. End-of-Month Estimation
    It predicts how much money the student is likely to have left by the end of the month.

    c. Early Warning Alerts
    If current spending trends suggest a possible shortage, the app warns users early so they can adjust their budget.

3. Subscription and Recurring Expense Monitoring
 The app tracks recurring payments such as streaming services, memberships, and software subscriptions. It highlights how small monthly charges accumulate into larger annual costs, helping students make informed decisions about which services are truly necessary.

  a. Recurring Payment Detection
  The app tracks repeated expenses such as streaming services, memberships, and software subscriptions.

  b. Cost Accumulation Display
  It shows how small monthly payments add up into significant yearly costs.

  c. Decision Support
  The app helps students decide which subscriptions are useful and which ones could be reduced or canceled.

4. Goal-Oriented Saving and Progress Tracking
 Students can set specific financial goals, such as saving for textbooks, rent, or personal expenses. The app visually tracks progress toward these goals and provides feedback that encourages consistent saving habits.

  a. Financial Goal Setting
  Students can create savings goals for needs like textbooks, rent, emergency funds, or personal purchases.

  b. Progress Visualization
  The app displays how much has been saved and how close the student is to reaching each goal.

  c. Saving Motivation
  It provides feedback and encouragement to help students stay consistent with their saving habits.
