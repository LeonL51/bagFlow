# bagFlow
A financial app that helps track expenses for college students 

# The Problem
After having conversations with multiple friends and through experience, I came to the conclusion that I am not the only person who used to have been stressed as a college student to have constantly overspent. Research also shows that 68 percent of the 53,158 students who have responded to a survey for fall 2024 SFWS Undergraduate Cohort have run out of money at least once since the start of the year. Additionally, approximately two-thirds of those students are working while enrolled in school. Moreover, over half have reported difficulties trying to juggle schoolwork and their financial stress(Trellis Strategies). 
	
The data reveals a rife issue: many students operate on limited and inconsistent income while managing tuition, rent, groceries, transportation, and recurring subscription services. Small expenses that go unnoticed and accumulate until there is a noticeable financial impact and stress. Without having a system to clearly visualize patterns, track expenses, and hold students accountable on their spending, students tend to make impulsive decisions that lead to unnecessary stress. 

Therefore, this app aims to address this problem by providing students a structured and proactive way to manage both income and expenses, while building healthier financial habits. 

# The Solution 
Instead of functioning as just another basic expense tracker, the app helps students clearly understand their spending habits and recognize patterns that contribute to financial stress. By organizing income, daily expenses, and recurring charges in one centralized space, students gain a clearer picture of where their money is going. Rather than reacting when their balance is already low, they can anticipate potential shortfalls in advance. The app highlights recurring subscriptions, demonstrates how small expenses accumulate over time, and encourages more intentional spending decisions. In addition, the app supports goal-oriented saving by allowing students to set realistic financial targets and track their progress. Through consistent feedback and predictive budgeting, students can understand how current spending choices may affect their finances later in the month or semester. Ultimately, the application shifts students from reacting to financial stress toward proactively managing their money, reducing anxiety and promoting greater financial control throughout their academic journey.
